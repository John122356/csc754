Group

John Lener

Topic

The use of modeling and simulation for creating AI benchmarks.

Description

Benchmarks are an important aspect of computer science and artificial intelligence(AI). They provide a way to empirically test and compare different models. In AI, however, simple static benchmarks can be gamed or suffer from the nature of having thousands of researchers try models only for some models to perform well by chance. Additionally, it can be argued that simple benchmarks do not test general intelligence as Francois Chollet argues in the paper "On the Measure of Intelligence".

For my paper I plan on writing a survey of Ai benchmarks that use modeling and simulation, because I think they must offer a way to address these problems. Dynamical benchmarks created by simulations are likely harder to game, while logic can be included in the benchmarks's model in order to test an AI model's capacity for reasoning logically.

Papers

  1. A Survey of Embodied AI: From Simulators to Research Tasks

  2. ABIDES: Towards High-Fidelity Market Simulation for AI Research

    This paper created an agent-based discrete event simulation environment that simulates financial markets for the purpose of ai research. This simulation environment can be used to study market phenomena and also the strategies of complex learning agents. Although the stated purpose of this simulation is to provide a platform for ai research, different ai agents are not tested in this paper.

  3. ABIDES-Gym: Gym Environments for Multi-Agent Discrete Event Simulation and Application to Financial Markets

    This paper uses the OpenAI Gym framework on top of the ABIDES financial market simulator to train and test reinforcement learning agents. Their framework is more general than only using the ABIDES simulator and can be used with any discrete event multi-agent simulator. They also introduce two financial market simulation environments to be used at reinforcement learning benchmarks.

  4. AgentBench: Evaluating LLMs as Agents

  5. AgentSims: An Open-Source Sandbox for Large Language Model Evaluation

    This paper advocates for using task as opposed to question and answering to evaluate LLM agents. This is to avoid common pitfalls of other LLM benchmarks such as data contamination and benchmark hacking. It provides an infrastructure for creating tasks for an LLM agent to perform in a simulated town.

  6. AI Safety Gridworlds

    This paper presents a suite of environments for testing the safety of reinforcement learning agents. These are cellular environments where the agent can interact with objects in the same cell or move to an adjacent cell. Each environment has a hidden safety function that scores the agent's performance on a different safety issue, such as if the agent learns to cheat for a reward.

  7. AI2-THOR: An Interactive 3D Environment for Visual AI

    AI2-THOR is a simulated environment that that offers a richer visual enviroment for learning agents than that of pictures and videos. It produces near photo realistic scenes through procedural generation with objects that can be interacted with. The Unity game engine provides a background that can return events based on the agent's actions. AI2-THOR seems to be a very popular framework for training and evaluating agent based AI.

  8. AirSim: High-Fidelity Visual and Physical Simulation for Autonomous Vehicles

    AirSim is a simulator for autonomous vehicles that has realistic visuals and physics, since real world data for autonomous vehicles is expensive and difficult to gather.

  9. Aligning Cyber Space with Physical World: A Comprehensive Survey on Embodied AI

    This is a survey paper about embodied AI, where an AI agent can interact with and learn from the physical world. Emobodied AI agents are often trained in a simulator before being brought into the real world, and this paper mentions many simulators. Some of which I have added to this list of papers.

  10. AlpacaFarm: A Simulation Framework for Methods that Learn from Human Feedback

    AlpacaFarm simulates human feedback to help train and evaluate LLM's abilities to follow instructions. This is to help developers experiment with different methods before investing in real human feedback. Since the simulations are replicable, different learning algorithms can be compared and several are tested in this paper. Its correctness is also validated by comparing it with real human feedback.

  11. ARC Prize 2024: Technical Report

    This paper is not about simulation and modeling, but is about a recent Kaggle competition using the ARC-AGI benchmark. This benchmark was created to test general intelligence by asking the system to solve novel tasks. Although it may not be appropriate for this survey paper, I think the ARC-AGI benchmark outlines why it's important to have more dynamical benchmarks for artificial intelligence.

  12. Autonomous Vehicle Evaluation: A Comprehensive Survey on Modeling and Simulation Approaches

    As the title suggests this paper covers many ways to simulate autonomous vehicles. On first glance I think the most interesting are the monte carlo simulations, because just about all of the other papers I have found are agent based simulations.
