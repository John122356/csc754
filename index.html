Group

John Lener

Topic

The use of modeling and simulation for creating AI benchmarks.

Description

Benchmarks are an important aspect of computer science and artificial intelligence(AI). They provide a way to empirically test and compare different models. In AI, however, simple static benchmarks can be gamed or suffer from the nature of having thousands of researchers try models only for some models to perform well by chance. Additionally, it can be argued that simple benchmarks do not test general intelligence as Francois Chollet argues in the paper "On the Measure of Intelligence".

For my paper I plan on writing a survey of Ai benchmarks that use modeling and simulation, because I think they must offer a way to address these problems. Dynamical benchmarks created by simulations are likely harder to game, while logic can be included in the benchmarks's model in order to test an AI model's capacity for reasoning logically.

Papers

  1. A Survey of Embodied AI: From Simulators to Research Tasks

    This is a survey paper on embodied AI where the AI agent has the ability to interact with the physical world. The physical world is often simulated for training and testing the agent, however, since it is easier and cheaper. This paper has pointed me in the direction of popular simulators, as well as being an example of a well written survey paper.

  2. ABIDES: Towards High-Fidelity Market Simulation for AI Research

    This paper created an agent-based discrete event simulation environment that simulates financial markets for the purpose of ai research. This simulation environment can be used to study market phenomena and also the strategies of complex learning agents. Although the stated purpose of this simulation is to provide a platform for ai research, different ai agents are not tested in this paper.

  3. ABIDES-Gym: Gym Environments for Multi-Agent Discrete Event Simulation and Application to Financial Markets

    This paper uses the OpenAI Gym framework on top of the ABIDES financial market simulator to train and test reinforcement learning agents. Their framework is more general than only using the ABIDES simulator and can be used with any discrete event multi-agent simulator. They also introduce two financial market simulation environments to be used at reinforcement learning benchmarks.

  4. AgentBench: Evaluating LLMs as Agents

  5. AgentSims: An Open-Source Sandbox for Large Language Model Evaluation

    This paper advocates for using task as opposed to question and answering to evaluate LLM agents. This is to avoid common pitfalls of other LLM benchmarks such as data contamination and benchmark hacking. It provides an infrastructure for creating tasks for an LLM agent to perform in a simulated town.

  6. AI Safety Gridworlds

    This paper presents a suite of environments for testing the safety of reinforcement learning agents. These are cellular environments where the agent can interact with objects in the same cell or move to an adjacent cell. Each environment has a hidden safety function that scores the agent's performance on a different safety issue, such as if the agent learns to cheat for a reward.

  7. AI2-THOR: An Interactive 3D Environment for Visual AI

    AI2-THOR is a simulated environment that that offers a richer visual enviroment for learning agents than that of pictures and videos. It produces near photo realistic scenes through procedural generation with objects that can be interacted with. The Unity game engine provides a background that can return events based on the agent's actions. AI2-THOR seems to be a very popular framework for training and evaluating agent based AI.

  8. AirSim: High-Fidelity Visual and Physical Simulation for Autonomous Vehicles

    AirSim is a simulator for autonomous vehicles that has realistic visuals and physics, since real world data for autonomous vehicles is expensive and difficult to gather.

  9. Aligning Cyber Space with Physical World: A Comprehensive Survey on Embodied AI

    This is a survey paper about embodied AI, where an AI agent can interact with and learn from the physical world. Emobodied AI agents are often trained in a simulator before being brought into the real world, and this paper mentions many simulators. Some of which I have added to this list of papers.

  10. AlpacaFarm: A Simulation Framework for Methods that Learn from Human Feedback

    AlpacaFarm simulates human feedback to help train and evaluate LLM's abilities to follow instructions. This is to help developers experiment with different methods before investing in real human feedback. Since the simulations are replicable, different learning algorithms can be compared and several are tested in this paper. Its correctness is also validated by comparing it with real human feedback.

  11. ARC Prize 2024: Technical Report

    This paper is not about simulation and modeling, but is about a recent Kaggle competition using the ARC-AGI benchmark. This benchmark was created to test general intelligence by asking the system to solve novel tasks. Although it may not be appropriate for this survey paper, I think the ARC-AGI benchmark outlines why it's important to have more dynamical benchmarks for artificial intelligence.

  12. Autonomous Vehicle Evaluation: A Comprehensive Survey on Modeling and Simulation Approaches

    As the title suggests this paper covers many ways to simulate autonomous vehicles. On first glance I think the most interesting are the monte carlo simulations, because just about all of the other papers I have found are agent based simulations.

  13. BabyAI: A Platform to Study the Sample Efficiency of Grounded Language Learning

    This is a grid-based environment where the AI agent must follow natural language instruction to complete a task such as “put the red ball next to the box on your left”. It uses a simulated teacher to give instruction and demonstrations which could potentially be replaced by a human to train the agent.

  14. BEHAVIOR-1K: A Human-Centered, Embodied AI Benchmark with 1,000 Everyday Activities and Realistic Simulation

    This benchmark simulates the activities that people would most like robots to be able to perform according to a survey conducted by the authors. It includes interactable objects with semantic annotation, realistic visuals, and physics, so a robot's performance in the simulation is more likely to translate to real life.

  15. Benchmarking Model Predictive Control and Reinforcement Learning Based Control for Legged Robot Locomotion in MuJoCo Simulation

    This paper uses the MuJoCo simulator to compare reinforcement learning with model predictive control algorithms for robot movement. MuJoCo is a physics simulator that seems to be popular.

  16. BetterBench: Assessing AI Benchmarks, Uncovering Issues, and Establishing Best Practices

    This paper is not about modeling and simulation, but provides a framework for evaluating benchmarks. Two issues it mentions that I think simulation based benchmarks can address are benchmark saturation and data contamination. Benchmark saturation is when the an AI model can perform nearly as well as is possible, and data contamination is when the data a model is trained on makes it into the test data. Complicated tasks in simulations that require planning and logic as well dynamically created environments can help address these issues.

  17. BOLAA: Benchmarking and Orchestrating LLM-augmented Autonomous Agents

    This paper aims to evaluate LLM agents both in terms of their agent architecture and their LLM backbones. These are experimented on in WebShop, a simulated website store, and HotpotQA, a question answering dataset.

  18. CARLA: An Open Urban Driving Simulator

    CARLA is an autonomous vehicle simulator. Simulations include static objects like roads, buildings, and plants and dynamic objects like cars and pedestrians as well as different weather and time of day conditions. Several different learning architectures are tested in this paper.

  19. CityEQA: A Hierarchical LLM Agent on Embodied Question Answering Benchmark in City Space

    CityEQA is a simulator for embodied agents to answer questions by exploring and interacting with the environment. This is a city environment as opposed to other simulators that generate indoor spaces. The scale of this bigger environment challenges the agent to explore lager areas while still considering the small scale for interactions.

  20. dm_control: Software and Tasks for Continuous Control

    Dm_control is a software suite for creating simulations that task an AI agent with controlling or manipulating objects. It uses the MuJoCo physics simulator.

  21. Evaluating Conversational Recommender Systems via User Simulation

    This paper is more detailed in its description of the modeling process than most of the other papers I have found. Most of the papers I have found just drop an agent into a simulated evironment to see what it can do.

  22. Gymnasium: A Standard Interface for Reinforcement Learning Environments

    Gymnasium is a library for creating reinforcement learning environments. It is an updated verion of OpenAI Gym and is used by many benchmarks to create simulated evironments.

  23. Habitat 2.0: Training Home Assistants to Rearrange their Habitat

    Habitat is a simulator that includes interactive artist created 3D apartment evironments, a physics simulator built for speed, and tasks for virtual robots to complete in these environments for the sake of benchmarking. There is also a Habitat 3.0 that includes virtual humanoids who can be can be swapped out of real humans using VR.

  24. Interactive Gibson Benchmark (iGibson 0.5): A Benchmark for Interactive Navigation in Cluttered Environments

    This paper presents iGibson, a benchmark and simulated environment that tests an agent's ability to navigate when interacting with obstacles in order to clear them is possible.

  25. LogiCity: Advancing Neuro-Symbolic AI with Abstract Urban Simulation

    This paper simulates cars and pedestrians in a city enviroment to use as a benchmark for neuro-symbolic AI.

  26. ManiSkill2: A Unified Benchmark for Generalizable Manipulation Skills

    This paper is similar to others in that it provides a 3D evironment for an embodied agent to interacts with. Its focus, however, is more on object manipulation as opposed to navigation. It provides a large variety of objects to manipulate including ones with soft-body physics, such as cloth.

  27. Meta-World: A Benchmark and Evaluation for Multi-Task and Meta Reinforcement Learning

    This benchmark contains a variety of manipulation tasks for an agent to perform in a simulated enviroment. Its goal is to spur development in multi-task learning, where an AI agent can perform well across many diverse tasks, and meta-learning, where an AI agent can generalize to perform well in previously unseen tasks.

  28. MineLand: Simulating Large-Scale Multi-Agent Interactions with Limited Multimodal Senses and Physical Needs

    This is a multi-agent simulator taking place in the video game Minecraft. It tests the agents' abilities to explore, cooperate, and compete while having limited senses of the evironment.

  29. Minigrid & Miniworld: Modular & Customizable Reinforcement Learning Environments for Goal-Oriented Tasks

    This paper presents a library for creating 2D or 3D grid-based environments for reinforcement learning. It supports multi-step tasks such as "use the key to open the door and then get to the goal".

  30. MuJoCo: A physics engine for model-based control

    MuJoCo is a physics engine used by many simulators.

  31. NovelGridworlds: A Benchmark Environment for Detecting and Adapting to Novelties in Open Worlds

    This benchmark focuses on injecting novel situations into a grid-based environment to test the planning and learning capabilities of an agent. It does this by changing the space of states and actions the agent can encounter.

  32. OpenAI Gym

    OpenAI Gym is a toolkit for making reinforcement learning environments and benchmarks. Many of the papers I have found use it, and it provides a consistent interface among them.

  33. PDEBENCH: An Extensive Benchmark for Scientific Machine Learning

    This is a benchmark for machine learning systems for scientific computing. What I think is interesting about this one is that the benchmark's simulations are numerically driven instead of being an agent in a virtual environment like most of the papers I have found.

  34. PettingZoo: Gym for Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning

    This is a toolkit for making reinforcement learning environments and benchmarks like OpenAI Gym, except it allows for multi-agent simulations instead of OpenAI Gym's single agent paradigm.

  35. PlasticineLab: A Soft-Body Manipulation Benchmark with Differentiable Physics

    This benchmark tests a simulated robot's ability to manipulate objects. Its simulated environment includes the soft-bodied physics of Plasticine, a clay like material, and differentiable physics for supervised learning with gradient-based optimization.

  36. ProcTHOR: Large-Scale Embodied AI Using Procedural Generation

    ProcTHOR is a framework for creating simulated evironments for training embodied AI. Its environments are procedurally generated, allowing it to produce signifantly more environments than similar frameworks.

  37. Retrospectives on the Embodied AI Workshop

    This is a summary of a workshop on embodied AI that has pointed me in the direction of popular simulators in the field.

  38. REVERIE: Remote Embodied Visual Referring Expression in Real Indoor Environments

    This benchmark uses the MatterPort3D simulator to test an embodied AI agent's ability to understand natural language, navigate, and ground objects. It does this with natual language instuctions to find an object in a simulated environment.

  39. Revisiting the Arcade Learning Environment: Evaluation Protocols and Open Problems for General Agents

    This paper discusses how The Arcade Learning Environment has been used by the reinforcement learning community. The Arcade Learning Environment is a popular platform for testing an AI agent's ability to play Atari video games. This paper seems to give more details on the modeling process than the original Arcade Learning Environment paper, such as how the environment is modeled as a Markov Decision Process.

  40. robosuite: A Modular Simulation Framework and Benchmark for Robot Learning

    Like many of the other papers, robosuite offers simulated evironments and tasks for a robot to perform in them. It has realistic physics using the MuJoCo simulator and supports procedural generation of environments.

  41. Safety-Gymnasium: A Unified Safe Reinforcement Learning Benchmark

    Using Gymnasium and MuJoCo, Safety-Gymnasium contains environments for reiforcement learning and benchmark tasks that emphasize safety.

  42. SimulBench: Evaluating Language Models with Creative Simulation Tasks

    SimulBench is a benchmark for testing an LLM's ability to simulate a user or scenario. It is not exactly what I'm looking for in these papers, but I thought it was interesting because it evaluates an LLM with a back and forth dialogue with GPT-4 which is almost its own kind of simulation.

  43. SocNavBench: A Grounded Simulation Testing Framework for Evaluating Social Navigation

    This is a benchmark for social navigation where a pedestrian agent must avoid obstacles such as other pedestrians. It provides a simulated envirnment with repeatable scenarios based on real pedestrian data and metrics to evaluate performance.

  44. SoundSpaces 2.0: A Simulation Platform for Visual-Acoustic Learning

    SoundSpaces is a sound simulator. It adds sound to a simulated 3D environment. This can be used for embodied AI agents to percept sound as well as in simulated NLP tasks.

  45. SUMMIT: A Simulator for Urban Driving in Massive Mixed Traffic

    SUMMIT is an extension of CARLA that simulates urban driving scenarios for the sake of training and testing autonomous vehicle algorithms. It provides an extra challenge by having unregulated scenarios such as intersections without traffic lights or stop signs.

  46. The Arcade Learning Environment: An Evaluation Platform for General Agents

    This is an influential paper that created a common interface to Atari games for reinforcement learning.

  48. TheAgentCompany: Benchmarking LLM Agents on Consequential Real World Tasks

  49. ThreeDWorld: A Platform for Interactive Multi-Modal Physical Simulation

  50. ToolAlpaca: Generalized Tool Learning for Language Models with 3000 Simulated Cases

  51. Visual Room Rearrangement

  52. VERIFAI: A Toolkit for the Design and Analysis of Artificial Intelligence-Based Systems

  53. Waymax: An Accelerated, Data-Driven Simulator for Large-Scale Autonomous Driving Research
